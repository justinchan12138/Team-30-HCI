    • User Requirements

    The primary goal of Human Computer Interaction (HCI) is to produce a usable system. A usable system:

    • Meets the users’ needs
    • Works in context of their use

    Systems under development should support users in achieving their goals. In order to produce a usable system, several methods can be used to establish users’ requirements. For our Alexa project, we compared and considered several different alternatives before choosing the ideal methods. Some of the methods which we considered are listed below:

    Technique Purpose Data type Advantages Disadvatanges
    Observation Understand the context of user’s activity without disturbing them Qualitative
  • Users don’t get distracted by data gathering
  • Automatic recording – extend over a long time period
  • Gives an insight that other techniques can’t give
  • Time consuming
  • Large amounts of data – requires tools to analyse and log
  • Participants may exaggerate
    • Survey Answering specific key questions Quantitative and Qualitative
  • Less resources needed, yet gather vast data from many people
  • Quantitative and statistic
  • Demographic
  • Crucial design
  • Low response rate unless designed carefully
  • Not in depth
  • Less chance to probe
    • Interview Exploring issues Some Quantitative, mostly Qualitative
  • Interviewees can be guided if required
  • Encourages contact between developers and users
  • Time-consuming
  • Artificial environment – not a realistic indication
  • May be intimidating for the interviewees, thus affecting the result


    • After making the above analysis, we decided to rule out ‘observation’. Even though getting plenty of data might be beneficial, it will be time-consuming to analyse them using the ideal tools. We also don’t have the sufficient resources and time to execute the analysis. It might also raise ethical issues, to an extent. Moreover, it only provides us with qualitative data whereas, quantitative data is also key for us. Hence, we felt that the cons outweigh the pros.
