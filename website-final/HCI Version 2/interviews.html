    • Interviews

    We also planned on carrying out an ‘interview’. We will show our hard-coded prototype to our Teaching Assistant and some of our classmates and ask for their feedback. We will do this during our lab session to manage time efficiently. We are also aware that interviews are intimidating and artificial. Hence, we will try to create an exceptionally relaxed atmosphere and make it more of a feedback session rather than an interview. It would allow us to explore the pros and cons of our prototype. We might even get a request for an Alexa Skill that we might have not considered otherwise. Some of the questions which we asked the users include:

    1. What do you think about the interaction of our prototype?
    2. Is it easy to use? How can we make it even more user-friendly?
    3. Are there any additional features which you would like to see in our product?
    4. Describe, in three words, your overall impression of our product.
    5. Is there anything else which you would like to tell us?
    The feedback which we received from this will be covered in the "Outcome of Evaluation" section.
